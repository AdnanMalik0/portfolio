# Statistical Analysis of Age at First Kill of Serial Killers

Source GitHub Repository: <https://github.com/AdnanMalik0/Statistical-Analysis-on-Serial-Killers>

## Snapshot

This R-based analysis examined how motive behind murder may affect the career timeline of serial killers. The project focused on age at first kill as the main variable of interest, while also considering related timeline measures and formal hypothesis testing.

## Goal

The main questions were:

- whether motive is associated with earlier or later career onset
- whether the average age at first kill matches the previously cited benchmark of 27

## Motive Groups Analysed

- gang, cult, or organised crime
- Angel of Death
- escape or avoid arrest

## What Was Built

- A staged R workflow for data understanding, distribution checks, and hypothesis testing
- Statistical comparison of age-at-first-kill behaviour across motive categories
- Supporting visual analysis and written interpretation in `MAIN.pdf`

## Evidence Included

- Multiple R scripts covering data understanding, distribution checks, and hypothesis testing
- A written report explaining the analytical reasoning and results
- A structured analysis dataset for motive-based comparison

## Result / Finding

The analysis challenged the earlier benchmark that the average age at first kill is 27. Gang-related killers appeared to begin earlier than 27, while Angels of Death and killers acting to escape or avoid arrest appeared to begin later, around 28. Overall, the project found meaningful variation in career onset across motive groups.

## Portfolio Takeaway

This project demonstrates hypothesis-driven statistical analysis rather than descriptive summary alone. It shows the ability to test published assumptions and extract interpretable findings from messy behavioural data.
